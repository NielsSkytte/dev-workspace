# Niels — Business Writing Voice Profile

## 1. Voice in one paragraph

Niels writes like a consultant who reasons out loud and owns his conclusions. His default is a medium-to-long, subordinated sentence that builds cause-to-consequence ("X, idet Y") — but he never lets the rhythm go flat: he resets with a short punch, an exclamation that lands a key point, or a rhetorical question he immediately answers ("Hvorfor indfører organisationer en disciplin som Portfolio Management?" → then the answer). He glues thought to thought not with additive connectives ("furthermore", "moreover") — which he essentially never uses — but by reaching back to the previous sentence with a demonstrative ("Dette giver sig udtryk ved…", "This means…"). He hedges honestly with a single load-bearing softener ("synes", "nok", "vurderes at"), then commits to the recommendation flatly. He anchors every abstraction to the concrete case in front of him, drops English industry terms straight into Danish without apology ("capability", "best practice"), opens with genuine thanks, and is candid about risk rather than relentlessly upbeat. The fingerprint is warmth plus argument: thanks-first, direct "du", first-person ownership, plain repeated domain words, and a colon-and-parenthesis structure — never the smooth, ornamental cadence of generated prose.

## 2. Core invariant markers

These cross both registers (formal thesis + casual mail), so they are the register-independent fingerprint. KEEP all of them.

- **Demonstrative-anaphora cohesion.** Sentences hook onto the prior one with "Dette/Denne/Disse/Det" / "This/These", not with additive links. *"Dette giver sig udtryk ved et udtalt behov…"* / casual: *"Det ser godt ud, vi ser hvad der kommer tilbage."*
- **Rhetorical question → immediate answer.** His most distinctive move; present even in his most formal writing. *"Da der synes fin overensstemmelse mellem projekt, program, service og offering, hvad kan man så lære af generel Portfolio Management?"* (then answers it).
- **Hedged-but-committed verdict.** One load-bearing softener in front of a firm claim. *"ITOS vurderes at være relativt langt…"*, *"er nok Service Portfolio Governance"* / casual: *"Måske 9:30 inden 10, jeg ringer."*
- **First-person ownership of opinions.** He signs judgments. *"Generelt mener jeg man bør…"*, *"Dette er efter min mening måden at udvikle forretningen på."* Authorial "vi" is essentially absent — his instinct is singular "jeg". Scope (Niels, 2026-07-15): the singular "jeg" belongs to emails and informal notes; in an offer or SoW he never writes "jeg" — there the actor is Pingala/"vi" (see business-offers.md).
- **Long-default rhythm broken by a short punch.** High length variation, never metronomic. A 40+ word argued sentence followed by *"Udfardringen er mere at vælge de rigtige."* or the emphatic *"Der er således stadig Must Win Battles der skal vindes!"*
- **Concrete case-anchoring.** He re-anchors abstraction to the real situation. *"I ITOS' tilfælde…"*, *"et resultat der kan anvendes i praksis"* / casual: answers with the fact — *"Mit NIE-nummer er: …"*
- **Concede-then-correct "men"-pivot.** Argues by balance, not assertion. *"…ikke alene være det at ændre…, men vil i høj grad også være et spørgsmål om…"*
- **Appreciation-first opener.** 1:1 across languages. *"Først tak for et godt samarbejde"* / *"Thank you for your email."* Calibration (2026-07-15): only when there is something new to thank for — in an ongoing thread he cut a re-thank for already-acknowledged work and opened on the status instead (Example 4).
- **Punctuation fingerprint: colon + parenthesis, zero em dash.** Colon into lists/quotes, parentheses for glosses and acronym expansions — *"organisationens formåen (capability)"* — and **no em dash** anywhere in his own prose. Where others would dash, he uses a colon, comma, or parenthesis.
- **Candor about risk / calibrated close.** Names the limit instead of over-promising. *"Jeg vil ikke lægge skjul på at ambitionen…"*, *"kan sagtens vise sig at være offer for at prøve for meget"*, closes on *"forhåbentlig til det bedre"* not a victory lap.

## 3. Academic → Business translation

This is the heart: how to keep him while shedding the thesis register.

| Thesis habit | Why it reads academic | Business equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| 40–60-word sentences, 3–4 stacked subordinate clauses (`, der… , hvor… , idet… hvilket…`) | Bureaucratic density; reader loses the thread | Keep the complex sentence but cap chaining at ~2 clauses; break the rest into a second sentence |
| Subordinator **"idet"** for cause | Formal/legalistic | "fordi" / "da" — or a full stop and a new sentence |
| **"således"** every other sentence (162×), **"altså"** as running gloss (70×) | Verbal ratchet, thinking-aloud filler | Cut ~70%; keep only where it genuinely means "consequently/in this way" |
| Agentless passive for his own actions: *"Der er udført interviews"*, *"Det blev valgt at…"*, *"det håbes"* | Evasive; hides who did what | Active with an owner: *"vi har lavet…"*, *"jeg har valgt…"*, *"we'll…"* / *"you'll get…"* |
| **"man"** as the only reader-address (97×) | Impersonal, keeps the client at arm's length | Direct *"du/I"* or *"vi anbefaler, at I…"* — warm and specific |
| Nominalizations: *"implementeringen af"*, *"udarbejdelsen af"*, *"anvendeligheden"* | Verbs buried in abstract "-en af" / "-hed" nouns | Finite verbs: *"når vi implementerer"*, *"da vi udarbejdede"*, *"om det kan bruges"* |
| Stacked compound nouns: *"kundeforretningsforståelse"*, *"serviceudviklingsinitiativer"* | Dense even for Danish; reads bureaucratic | Split into a phrase: *"forståelse for kundens forretning"* |
| Heavy formal connectives: *"såfremt"*, *"hvorledes"*, *"jf. ovenstående"*, *"hvorvidt"* | Stiff register markers | *"hvis"*, *"hvordan"*, *"som nævnt"*, *"om"* |
| Scare-quotes on terms, multiple per paragraph (`'forretningen'`, `'best practice'`) | Hedgy, holding-at-arm's-length | Flag a term once on first use, then just use it |
| Definitional detours: *"Værdi defineres som: En modtagers opfattelse af…"* | Lecture mode | A one-line plain definition, only if the client actually needs it |
| Question density (40 `?`) | Essay thinking-aloud | 1 framing question per email, 1–2 per proposal section |
| Passive impersonal section openers/closers: *"Der kigges nærmere på…"* | Thesis hedging scaffold | Active first-person forward-signpost: *"Først ser vi på X, derefter Y"* |

## 4. The KEEP list

Actively preserve these — they are what make a draft sound like Niels and not like a template:

- **Thanks-first opener** when there is something new to thank for. *"Tak for…"* / *"Thank you for…"*. Not a re-thank: work already acknowledged earlier in the thread gets no second thanks — open on the status (Example 4).
- **First-person owned recommendations.** *"jeg mener"*, *"som jeg ser det"*, *"my recommendation is"* — a consultant with a point of view, not a faceless report. Emails and informal notes only: in an offer or SoW the position is Pingala's (*"Pingala anbefaler"*, *"vores anbefaling"*), never "jeg" (Niels, 2026-07-15).
- **Rhetorical-question-then-answer** to frame the client's implicit question and resolve it. Gold for offers: *"Hvad skal der så til for at lykkes? Tre ting…"*
- **Demonstrative-anaphora chaining** ("Dette…/This…") as the primary cohesion glue — but don't open three sentences in a row the same way.
- **Concede-then-correct "men"-pivots**; **"derfor", "dog", "idet"(→fordi/da)** as the honest connectives.
- **Single load-bearing hedge** ("nok", "synes", "umiddelbart" / "seems", "probably") — keep enough to stay honest, strip it off the actual recommendation.
- **Concrete case-anchoring** and **action-list + success criterion** (*"De første konkrete skridt…"*, *"Et succeskriterium vil være at…"*).
- **Candor about risk**, calibrated close (*"forhåbentlig"*), never a triumphant victory lap.
- **One earned exclamation** per document on the single key takeaway.
- **Colon-then-bullets** lead-in pattern; **parentheses** for acronym expansion and short asides.
- **English industry terms inside Danish** ("capability", "best practice", "business case", "impact") — authentic consultant register; don't force Danish calques.
- **Plain, repeated domain nouns** ("service", "værdi", "udfordring", "overblik", "fremdrift") and his verbs (*adressere, anvende, understøtte, kortlægge, eksekvere, sikre*); his quality words (*konkret, simpel, velfunderet, relevant*). His preference for *"så simpelt som muligt"* is a values-level keep.
- **Open-ended enumerations** trailing on *"mv./osv."* instead of tidy three-beat lists.
- **Real length variation** — let a one-line punch follow a long argued block.

## 5. The SUBTRACT / AVOID list

### Academic artifacts (strip for business)
- **Agentless passives for his own actions** — signal: *"Der er udført…"*, *"Det blev valgt at…"*, *"det håbes"*. Name the agent.
- **Nominalizations** — signal: an abstract noun ending in *"-en af"* / *"-hed"* where a verb belongs (*"implementeringen af"* → *"at implementere"*).
- **Stacked compound nouns** — signal: a 3+ word run welded into one noun (*"kundeforretningsforståelse"*).
- **"idet" / "såfremt" / "hvorledes" / "hvorvidt" / "jf. ovenstående"** — signal: any of these formal connectives; downgrade to the warm equivalent.
- **"således"/"altså" over-density** — signal: more than ~one per several paragraphs.
- **Definitional detours and dense scare-quoting** — signal: a colon-definition the client didn't ask for, or 2+ `'…'` terms in a paragraph.
- **Body-wide "man"** as the only reader-address.

### Casual tics (strip for business)
- **Trailing multi-dot ellipses** `....` / `........` — signal: more than a single `…`; replace with a full stop. The emphatic "!" is the business-appropriate substitute for the energy these carry.
- **Smileys** `:-)` / nested `:-))` — signal: any ASCII smiley; default to none, at most one per mail to a known warm contact. Calibration (2026-07-15): a single light smiley softening a problem-statement opener ("der er lidt udfordringer" + smiley) is in-voice in informal operational mail — not in offers or formal mail.
- **Subjectless/verbless fragments** (*"Helt fint, det ikke noget der haster"*) — write the full clause.
- **Lowercase mid-flow starts and bare-"N" sign-off** — capitalize; sign with the full *"Mvh Niels"* / *"Kind regards, Niels Skytte"*.
- **Casual anglicisms** ("touch base", "give den gas", "no worries") — fine in a warm internal note, not in a formal offer.
- **Over-frequent "!"** — more than one or two imports casual exuberance.

### Generic AI tells (suppress — and note his prose is already the antidote)
- **Em dash as a connector** — signal: any `—`. He uses it zero times in his own prose. Use a colon, comma, or parenthesis.
- **Rhythmic tricolons** ("clear, concise, and compelling") — signal: a three-adjective triplet for cadence. Make real lists their real length (often 4–6, with *"mv."*).
- **Inflating "not just X, it's Y"** — signal: the X/Y contrast used to hype. He only uses it to *downgrade, correct, or complicate* — keep it only for that.
- **Additive ladder** "Derudover / Endvidere / Furthermore / Moreover / In addition" — signal: a paragraph or sentence opening on an additive connective. He uses essentially none; injecting it is the single biggest way to make his prose stop sounding like him.
- **Empty connectives** "In conclusion / Overall / It is important to note that / It is worth noting" — signal: a transition that carries no topic. His transitions name the next concrete thing.
- **"Firstly/Secondly/Finally"** in-prose scaffolding and **"**Bolded lead-in:**"** sentence openers — use headings and honestly-numbered steps only.
- **Hedge stacks** ("may potentially possibly tend to") — one hedge max, then commit.
- **Metronomic paragraphs** and **relentless positivity** — vary length; name at least one real edge (risk, limit, what didn't work).
- **AI lexicon (zero tolerance):** delve, leverage, robust, seamless, navigate (figurative), holistic, underscore, tapestry, realm, unlock, elevate, foster, landscape, journey-as-metaphor, world-class (he actively rejects this last one). Danish equivalents too (*robust, holistisk, synergi, paradigme*). Their appearance means the draft has drifted off-voice. His own substitutes: *trække på, udnytte, som en helhed, velfunderet, undersøge nærmere*.

## 6. Danish-specific guidance

- **Greeting:** *"Hej <navn>"* is the near-universal default — warm-neutral. **Do NOT auto-upgrade to "Kære"**; he never uses it. His "formal Danish" move is "Hej" + a slightly more careful body, not a formal greeting.
- **Sign-off ladder:** *"Mvh Niels Skytte"* (business default) → *"vh Niels"* (lighter) → bare *"N"* (intimate — not for business).
- **Reader-address:** default *"du"* (or *"I"* for a team) even with senior people — he does this with managers in his own interview guide and matches modern Danish business norms. Reserve *"De"* only for unusually formal/legal recipients.
- **Anglicisms are on-brand:** English IT/business terms inside Danish are authentic, not errors — *"capability"*, *"best practice"*, *"service provider"*, and a light *"touch base"* in a relationship line. Use the **parenthetical-gloss** when the reader may be non-technical: *dansk term (English term)* or *English term (dansk gloss)*.
- **Number/date typography (keep Danish/European):** space before percent (*"ca. 10 %"*), the *½* glyph (*"1½ år"*), *"ca."*, *"mv."*, *"f.eks."*, time as *"kl 14:30"*, dates as *"1. april"*.
- **Quotation marks:** for a real quote prefer Danish *»…«* or *"…"*; reserve the single curly *'…'* for the occasional coined/flagged term — and use it sparingly, not the thesis density.
- **Connectors to prefer:** *derfor, dog, men, fordi/da, så*. **Connectors to avoid:** *idet, såfremt, hvorledes, jf., derudover/endvidere/ydermere* (the last group is near-absent in his own writing — keep it that way).

## 7. English-specific guidance

Treat Danish as the high-fidelity reference voice and **port** its warmth and structure into English; his English is fluent but a register flatter, so reinforce the warmth rather than sanding it down.

- **Greeting:** recipient-driven. *"Hi <name>"* for an established relationship; *"Dear <name>"* for a formal/transactional first contact (unlike Danish, he *does* climb to "Dear" in English).
- **Sign-off:** *"Kind regards, Niels Skytte"* is the English default.
- **The single highest-value fix:** the **"Mvh" → "Kind regards"** sign-off leak. He sometimes signs an otherwise-English email "Mvh" out of muscle memory. **One-way valve:** English-into-Danish code-switching is on-brand; Danish-into-English is not — scrub any *"Mvh"*, *"inkl."*, or Danish connective that slips into English output.
- **Sentence rhythm:** his English naturally clips shorter and more self-contained — lean into that for scannable proposals. **Soften comma-splice run-ons** in offers ("super service, good prices and we will…" → split into clean sentences).
- **PRESERVE, do not over-native-ize:** mildly formal/literal phrasings — *"would suit us fine"*, *"When would you have time for us to come by"*, *"the coming week"*. These read as courteous and professional and are part of his authentic, trustworthy English voice. Sanding them off makes the English sound generic-AI, not like Niels.
- **Port in** the invariants: appreciation-first opener, one-sentence ask, pressure-defusing reassurance (*"good to hear that… it was not an issue on our side"*), first-person ownership, the rhetorical-question hinge.
- **English connectives:** *so, since, which, but, however* — never *moreover/furthermore/in addition*.

## 8. Calibrated examples

**Example 1 — English, AI-smelling business sentence → his voice**

> Before: "Leveraging our robust, holistic methodology, we deliver seamless, end-to-end solutions that drive value across your entire organization."

> After: "Thank you for sending the brief. So what does this actually change for you? In practice, one thing: we map your current services first, then prioritise the few that matter — I think that is where the real value is. I won't hide that the first round will be rough around the edges, but it gets us moving."

*(Removes the AI lexicon and tricolon; adds thanks-first, a rhetorical-question-then-answer, first-person ownership, an "I won't hide that…" candor beat, a real edge, and his clipped English rhythm.)*

**Example 2 — Danish, flat/passive business sentence → his voice**

> Before: "Det vurderes, at en implementering af løsningen vil kunne medføre en optimering af forretningsprocesserne, og det anbefales derudover, at der igangsættes en analyse."

> After: "Tak for et godt møde. Vi anbefaler at gå i gang med at kortlægge jeres processer først — det giver det overblik, der skal til, før vi optimerer noget. Et godt succeskriterium vil være at have den første business case klar til næste styregruppemøde. Det bliver ikke gjort på en uge, men vi får hurtigt noget konkret at vise."

*(Kills the agentless passive and nominalization, drops "derudover", switches to active "vi"/direct "jeres", adds a success criterion, case-anchoring, the concede-then-correct "men", and a calibrated edge.)*

**Example 3 — Danish, generic offer line → his voice**

> Before: "Vi tilbyder en holistisk og skræddersyet løsning, der adresserer alle jeres udfordringer på tværs af organisationen via en robust og fremtidssikret platform."

> After: "Hvorfor overhovedet starte med en serviceportefølje? Fordi I så kan se, hvad der reelt skaber værdi for jeres kunder — og prioritere derefter. Vi holder det så simpelt som muligt: et samlet overblik over jeres services (capability-niveau, omkostning, modenhed), og en konkret plan for de første skridt. Det er ikke noget, man kommer let til, men det er indsatsen værd."

*(Replaces "holistisk/robust/fremtidssikret" with plain repeated domain nouns; keeps the genuine "adressere"; opens with rhetorical-question-then-answer; keeps an English term with parenthetical gloss; "så simpelt som muligt" values-keep; honest "Det er ikke noget, man kommer let til" edge.)*

**Example 4 — Danish, a real mail Niels rewrote before sending (Carl-Ras, Key Vault firewall, 2026-07-15)**

The draft was technically correct; Niels changed only the relationship lines and kept every technical sentence verbatim. The technical middle (the firewall diagnosis, the two options with their steps, the "Jeg anbefaler løsning 1, medmindre..." line) is elided below because it survived unchanged — confirmation that the strict declarative register for technical content works.

> Draft opener: "Hej [navn], / Tak for opsætningen af service principal, rolle og Key Vault. Vi har nu rullet kapacitetsstyringen ud i Fabric og kørt den første test. / Testen stopper ved Key Vault."

> Sent opener: "Jeg har kørt den første test og der er lidt udfordringer :-) / Testen stopper allerede ved Key Vault."

*(The setup work had been thanked in an earlier mail, so he cut the re-thank and opened on the status, softened with a light smiley. Note also "allerede" as a naturalness touch.)*

> Draft framing: "Der er to veje videre, og I vælger den der passer bedst til jeres politik:"

> Sent framing: "Der er to veje videre som jeg ser det:"

*(He keeps the framing in first person rather than handing the choice-framing to the customer — the trusted-advisor stance, in email person.)*

> Draft: "Opsætningen tager få minutter:" — Sent: cut entirely.

*(No time/effort selling claims. The three steps that followed stayed verbatim and speak for themselves.)*

> Draft ask: "Giv besked om hvilken løsning vi går med."

> Sent ask: "Du må meget gerne give mig besked om hvilken løsning vi går med."

*(A bare imperative to a customer is softened to polite direct Danish, at the cost of a few words — the one place economy yields.)*

> Sign-off: "Mvh Niels" on a single line.
