Data Excellence — Maturity Journey

The 6 steps to becoming truly data driven, and how to avoid wasting resources on things that doesn't really matter

Step Plateau How it begins Why it stalls
1 – Automate The reporting plateau The reporting required for a high-level historical descriptive and understanding of the organizations is created and automized allowing for cost savings and increased data accessibility The impact plateaus as the reporting is unaligned to strategy or specific initiatives and doesn't exceed the high-level understanding. Thus, giving limited actionable insights, for most of the decision makers. Impact relies heavily on the judgments of single members the management team. Meanwhile, costs can escalate endlessly as new demands emerge for; higher data accuracy, increased number of reports, better reporting experiences, or expanded data platform capabilities
2 – Align & elaborate The mental model plateau The high-level reports are gradually expanded into a set of analytical reports, aligned with strategy or initiatives. This enables decision makers to make somewhat informed decisions in relation to strategy trough a manual process. The analytical scope is often confined to a fixed mental model that has not been properly tested or validated. As a result, even when decision makers seek to inform themselves, their decisions risk being biased or inconsistent rather than genuinely data-driven.
3 – Validate The analytical plateau The analytical scope expands, enabling the identification and validation of clear patterns. This allows decision makers to base their decision on more reliable and data driven insights. Despite the sophistication of the analytics, they are not systematically embedded into decision-making. Insights remain siloed, overly complex, or too slow to shape day-to-day operations. The organizational chaos prevents decisions from being made in a consistently informed way
4 – Adopt The action plateau Our complex analytics are widely adopted by throughout the organizations and are utilized for slow paced deliberate decision processes. The interplay between decisions that are either high-volume (e.g., pricing thousands of products) or high-speed (e.g., automated real-time trading) and the bounded resources of humans limits the organization's ability to fully leverage its data.
5 – Integrate The experimental plateau Complex analytics are fully integrated within our systems landscape allowing for fully automated optimized decision making. Organizations are unwilling to use their data capabilities to perform experiments to optimize their business. Typically, due to risk-adverse management system and lack of experimental culture
6 – Transform The market plateau The organization is able to use data to transform the entire organizations and achieving market leadership possibly within a lucrative market identified and chosen based on data. External market factors
