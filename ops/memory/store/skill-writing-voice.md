`.claude/skills/writing-voice/` governs the **voice** of any prose drafted for Niels (emails,
offers, docs), in Danish or English. Built by mining his **Danish master's thesis** (~33k words,
the deep voice DNA) and his **personal Gmail sent-mail** (the natural, unguarded register), then
translating academic -> business.

The method is **subtraction-first**: strip the AI tells, then bend what remains toward his markers.
His confirmed fingerprint: em dash = **0** in his own prose, no additive ladder
(derudover/furthermore/moreover), thanks-first opener, first-person owned recommendations, one
load-bearing hedge then commit, demonstrative cohesion ("Dette.../This..."), candor about risk.

Structure: `SKILL.md` (philosophy + register map + the AI-tell AVOID list + an 8-point self-check)
plus references `voice-profile.md` (the evidence-backed fingerprint), `business-offers.md` (the
strict offer layer), `danish.md`, `english.md`. The reader is **always a Danish/Nordic business
customer** (often a non-native English reader). Composes with `pingala-offer` (offer stance),
`fabric-licensing` (facts), and `email-outlook-ready` (formatting). See [[skill-pingala-offer]].

**Two universal rules added 2026-06-29** (from Niels editing the Melbye SoW; now in SKILL.md
universal rules + self-check 6b):
- **No breezy or "clever" phrasing.** Register is formal Nordic/Danish business language, not
  American sales punch. Kill quotable/wry lines ("built in an afternoon", "the magic happens
  here"); state the fact plainly. Plain and factual beats catchy.
- **Label/heading words must name the verifiable property, not a vibe.** A title or bold label
  says what the thing *is*, checkably: "a validated data agent" (grounded, tested), not "a
  proper/real/serious agent". If you can't say what the word verifies, it's the wrong word.
