When making a **major revision** to a versioned client-facing document (Statement of Work, offer, proposal, contract), **bump the version number and add a row to the Document History table** describing the change — without being asked. A major revision = a structural change, scope/phase change, or estimate change; not a typo or wording tweak.

**Why:** these documents go to clients and get signed; the version + change history is how both sides track what changed between drafts and avoid arguing over which version is current.

**How to apply:** after a substantive edit, update the Document History table (Version, Change summary, Author, Date — convert relative dates to absolute). Author defaults to the Pingala consultant (Niels Skytte) unless stated. Use semantic-ish draft versions (0.1, 0.2, … pre-signature; 1.0 at first signed/issued). Mention the bump in your summary. Relates to the SoW pipeline [[fill-sow]] — its Q5 handles the same bump at the Word-render step; this rule covers the authoring (markdown) side so the two stay in sync.
